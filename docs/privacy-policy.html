Privacy Policy

Khammen · Last updated 22 July 2026

Khammen is a party word game. It has no accounts, no advertising, and no analytics. The only thing you tell it about yourself is the display name you type before joining a room.

What we do not collect

  • No account, sign-up, email address, phone number or password.
  • No advertising, ad identifiers (IDFA), or third-party ad networks.
  • No analytics, crash-reporting or tracking SDKs of any kind.
  • No access to your camera, microphone, location, contacts, photos or calendar — the app requests no device permissions at all.
  • We do not sell, rent or share personal data with anyone.

What is stored on your device

The app saves three small values locally so the game behaves sensibly between sessions. These never leave your device except as described in the next section:

  • teamtaboo:name — the display name you last used, so you don't retype it.
  • teamtaboo:session — which room you were in, so that closing the app mid-game and reopening it puts you back in the same room as the same player.
  • teamtaboo:muted — whether you turned sound off.

Deleting the app removes all three.

What is sent to the game server

Khammen is multiplayer, so play has to pass through a server. While you are in a room, the app sends:

  • the display name you chose;
  • the room code you created or joined;
  • the guesses you type during a turn, which the server scores;
  • game actions — team assignments, team names, turn length and target score, and starting or restarting a game;
  • a temporary player identifier generated for that room, used only to reconnect you.

No other information is transmitted. The app never asks for and never sends anything that identifies you personally beyond the name you freely choose.

What other players can see

This is a group game, so treat anything you type as visible to the room. Other players in your room see your display name, your team, your score, and — during a turn — the guesses you submit, including which player solved a word. Choose a nickname rather than your full name if you would prefer not to be identifiable.

How long data is kept

Game rooms are temporary. A room exists on the server for the duration of the game and is discarded afterwards; we do not build profiles, keep play history, or retain your name after a room ends. Leaving a room removes you from it immediately.

Infrastructure and third parties

The game server runs on Cloudflare, which processes the connection on our behalf as an infrastructure provider. Distribution and any beta testing are handled by Apple through the App Store and TestFlight, under Apple's own privacy policy. We use no other third-party services.

Children

Khammen is a general-audience game and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because a player's display name is visible to everyone in the room, we encourage younger players to use a nickname rather than their real name.

Your choices

  • Play under any nickname you like — nothing verifies it.
  • Leave a room at any time to remove yourself and your name from it.
  • Uninstall the app to erase everything it stored on your device.
